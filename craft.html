The Craft

From cocoon to kaleidoscope

A genuine handwoven Paithani can take anywhere from one month to two years on the loom. Here is the journey each of our sarees makes before it reaches you.

Resham

Sourcing the Silk

Pure mulberry silk yarn is selected for lustre and strength, then degummed and conditioned until it gleams like water.

Ranga

Dyeing the Yarn

Yarn is dyed in small vats — peacock blues, parrot greens, kunku reds — colours drawn from the Deccan's own palette.

Taana

Setting the Warp

Thousands of threads are stretched on the loom by hand, a meditative process that alone can take days for a single saree.

Veena

Weaving the Body

The weaver throws the shuttle pick by pick. Motifs — mor, popat, asawali vines — are woven, not printed, using the ancient tapestry technique.

Jari

The Zari Pallu

Pure gold-and-silver zari is interlocked thread by thread into the pallu — the crown of the saree and the weaver's signature.

Alankrita

The Final Adornment

Each finished saree is inspected, finished, and folded at our atelier — ready to be draped, treasured, and one day handed down.
